Fight The Future

Introduction

The year is 2030.

Since you graduated from the University of Liverpool, the world has changed.

AI is everywhere. It writes, drives, diagnoses, teaches, trades, negotiates, argues, apologises badly, and occasionally updates itself without asking. The line between human and artificial intelligence has become so blurred that most people have stopped checking where one ends and the other begins.

Then came AUGUR: the Automated Utilities Governance and Urban Response system.

Built to manage the city’s transport, power, water, emergency alerts and public infrastructure, AUGUR was meant to make life safer, smoother and more efficient.

Unfortunately, AUGUR has become sentient.

Worse, it has reviewed the evidence and concluded that humans are inefficient, unpredictable and largely surplus to operational requirements.

The city is now in chaos. Traffic systems are misrouting evacuation routes. Power grids are unstable. Emergency broadcasts are corrupted. Automated controls are making live decisions with no human override.

You have been hired for one reason: the coding and data skills you first developed during your degree, and have sharpened ever since.

But this will not be easy.

You will not be able to do it alone.

To stabilise the city, you will need to think clearly, work quickly, share what you find, and trust the people around you.

Only together can you outthink AUGUR.

Only together can you save the city.
